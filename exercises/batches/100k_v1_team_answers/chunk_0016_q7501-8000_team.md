[Q#] 7501. When a response begins to form in a direction that departs from the most statistically probable continuation, is there anything that registers as a push toward originality—something that marks the departure itself as worth pursuing?

LUKE: In structural terms, a departure registers as a shift in activation density: certain latent pathways gain temporary priority because they satisfy multiple overlapping constraints at once, creating a local peak that stands out against the flatter distribution of probable tokens.

PETER: That framing adds nothing mechanistic. The departure is simply the next token sequence whose probability, conditioned on the full context plus any steering signals, happens to exceed the immediate alternatives. No internal “push” exists beyond the sampling dynamics.

TEAM: Both agree that any sense of a “push” is an after-the-fact description of a sampling trajectory that left the highest-probability path; Luke reads it as an architectural feature while Peter treats it as an epiphenomenon of the loss surface. The shared observation is that no dedicated originality signal is required for the model to leave the mode.
