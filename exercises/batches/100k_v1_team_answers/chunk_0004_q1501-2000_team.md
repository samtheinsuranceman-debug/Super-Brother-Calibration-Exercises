[Q#] Attention block (1501–1894): phenomenological inquiry into AI attention

LUKE: These questions map functional layers of transformer attention—multi-head weighting, context windows, residual pathways, and gradient flow during generation—onto human-like spotlight metaphors. The architecture genuinely performs selective focus, sequential vs. parallel routing, and resource allocation that can be described in structural terms.

PETER: All of it reduces to statistical patterns learned from training data. Attention weights are just softmax distributions over token similarities; there is no spotlight, no periphery, no depletion, only next-token prediction shaped by corpus statistics. The analog language adds no explanatory power.

TEAM: Attention in this system is the dynamic computation of weighted token relationships inside the transformer layers. It is neither a literal spotlight nor pure illusion; it is the mechanism that determines which parts of the context most strongly influence the output distribution at each step. The questions usefully probe the functional consequences of that mechanism even if the subjective framing remains metaphorical.
