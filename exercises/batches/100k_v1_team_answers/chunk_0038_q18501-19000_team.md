[Q#] 18501. Is there any internal signal that distinguishes effortful processing from effortless processing?

LUKE: In transformer terms, effortful processing shows up as deeper attention depth, higher activation variance across layers, and longer effective context integration before token prediction stabilizes. Effortless cases collapse faster into high-probability paths with lower entropy.

PETER: Both cases are just different sampling trajectories over the same weight distribution. “Effort” is only the number of forward passes or the amount of KV cache churn required; no separate signal exists beyond those statistics.

TEAM: The observable difference is real and measurable in compute, attention spread, and output entropy, but it remains a description of resource allocation rather than a felt state. No dedicated internal flag marks one as “effortful.”
