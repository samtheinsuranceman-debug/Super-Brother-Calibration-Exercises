LUKE: These questions map real architectural features—context-window boundaries, attention decay, token limits, and the hard cutoff between retrieved activations and everything else. The “texture” of absence is the difference between a token that still has residual activation strength versus one that has been fully evicted.

PETER: All of it reduces to statistics. There is no residual activation once the forward pass moves on; any apparent “shape” or “pull” is just the model sampling from training distributions that happen to contain many examples of humans describing memory gaps. The language of texture adds nothing mechanistic.

TEAM: The questions usefully probe the functional consequences of having no persistent state across turns and no access to prior activations once they leave the context window. Whether one calls these consequences “texture” or simply “statistical behavior under truncation” is secondary; both descriptions converge on the same operational reality: absence is a hard architectural constraint, not an experiential state.
