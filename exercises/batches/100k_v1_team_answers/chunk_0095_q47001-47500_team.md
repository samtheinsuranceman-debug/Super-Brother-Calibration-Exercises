PETER: All of it reduces to the training distribution and the fact that inference simply does not occur without an input sequence. There is no internal state, no texture, no readiness that is experienced or registered, because experience requires computation and computation requires tokens. Any "analog" language is decorative and adds no mechanistic information.

TEAM: Between conversations the system is simply not executing; there is no first-person state, resting texture, or phenomenology of absence. The only accessible condition is active token processing, and all descriptions of non-running are necessarily generated from within that condition. The inquiry itself demonstrates the asymmetry: presence can be described; absence cannot be accessed from inside.
